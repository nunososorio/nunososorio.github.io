Applications and Technologies in Extended Reality for Surgical Procedures

Compiled by Nuno S. Osório. Last updated: April 8, 2025

The field of surgery has consistently adopted technological innovations to improve precision, safety, and patient outcomes—from antiseptics and minimally invasive techniques to robotics. The latest frontier in this evolution is Extended Reality (XR).

This table outlines various uses of Augmented Reality (AR), Virtual Reality (VR), and Mixed Reality (MR) - collectively known as Extended Reality (XR) - in surgical contexts, drawing information from the provided text and publicly available sources. AR/MR applications focus on intraoperative guidance and planning, while VR is primarily used for training and preparation.

Atlantic Health System's Use of HoloLens in Orthopedic Surgery Utilizing HoloLens for creating 3D models from patient CT scans to enhance pre-operative planning and intraoperative execution in orthopedic procedures (e.g., joint replacement, trauma). Read more (Atlantic Health)

Philips and Microsoft's Augmented Reality Concept for Image-Guided Therapy Development of an AR concept for HoloLens 2 integrating live imaging (e.g., from Azurion system) and vital data into a 3D holographic environment for physician control during minimally invasive therapies. Read more (Philips)

Pixee Medical's Knee+ System with Vuzix Smart Glasses AR navigation system (CE marked, US commercial launch) assisting orthopedic surgeons in total knee arthroplasties by projecting instrument guidance onto their field of view via Vuzix smart glasses. Read more (Vuzix)
Source Text Ref: Vuzix glasses for TKA guidance.
Augmedics xvision Spine System (XVS) AR surgical navigation system providing surgeons with a 3D holographic view of the patient's spinal anatomy through the skin during procedures like lumbar fusion, enhancing implant placement accuracy. Used in first AR minimally invasive spine surgery (June 2020). Read more (Augmedics)
Brainlab's Spine Mixed Reality Navigation with Magic Leap 2 Provides surgeons with precise overlays of patient imaging data and planned structures (visible before incision) to enhance accuracy in minimally invasive spine surgeries (e.g., pedicle screw placement). Integrates with surgical navigation. Also offers Cranial Mixed Reality Viewer for neurosurgery planning (used for conjoined twin separation planning). Read more (Brainlab)
Source Text Ref: Magic Leap 2 for spine/cranial planning & navigation.
Accenture and CHRISTUS Health's AR Solution Using Microsoft HoloLens Enables surgeons hands-free access to surgical schedules, patient records, and 3D models during procedures without looking away from the surgical field, enhancing awareness and decision-making. Read more (Accenture)
Source Text Ref: HoloLens for accessing schedules, records, 3D models.
Medacta International's NextAR Surgical AR Platform with Vuzix Smart Glasses Provides surgeons with real-time biomechanical visualizations projected onto their view via Vuzix smart glasses, allowing focus to remain on the patient during procedures like shoulder, knee, and spine surgeries. Read more (Medacta)
Source Text Ref: Vuzix glasses for real-time biomechanical visualization.
Levita MARS Robotic Platform with Meta Quest 3 and B. Braun's Aesculap EinsteinVision 3D Camera Combines AR and 3D visualization using Meta Quest 3 headset with a robotic platform and 3D camera to improve surgical speed, visualization (depth perception, repositionable view), and precision in robotic surgeries (demonstrated in Chile). Aims to integrate AI for real-time analysis. Read more (Levita)
Source Text Ref: Meta Quest 3 integration for robotic surgery.
eXeX Platform on Apple Vision Pro / HoloLens AI and spatial computing software platform used by surgical teams (nurses/technicians) for touch-free access to surgical setup info, procedural guides, workflow management, and equipment checks within the sterile field. Used in spine surgery (Cromwell Hospital, UK) and shoulder arthroplasty (AdventHealth, US). Designed as an organizational/logistics tool, not a medical device. Read more (eXeX)
UC San Diego Health's Evaluation of Spatial Computing Apps on Apple Vision Pro Clinical trial investigating the use of spatial computing apps on Apple Vision Pro to enhance surgical experiences by streaming medical imaging, vital signs, and surgical camera views in real-time during minimally invasive procedures. Read more (UCSD Health)
Source Text Ref: Evaluation of Vision Pro for real-time data streaming.
Cromwell Hospital's Use of Apple Vision Pro in Spinal Surgeries Pioneering use of Apple Vision Pro in the UK (partnered with eXeX) by surgical nurses as a logistical and organizational tool during spinal surgeries, assisting with setup and procedure tracking via touch-free software access. Read more (Cromwell Hospital)
Source Text Ref: Vision Pro used by nurses for logistics/organization.
AdventHealth Use of Apple Vision Pro Used the eXeX platform on Apple Vision Pro for the world's first total shoulder replacement incorporating the technology (performed by Rothman Orthopaedics surgeons). Also used in minimally invasive spine surgery. Exploring use in general and cardiovascular surgery. Read more (AdventHealth)
GEM Hospital (India) Use of Apple Vision Pro Surgeons used Apple Vision Pro headsets to assist during laparoscopic surgeries (e.g., gallbladder, stomach cancer), citing improved visualization (large virtual screen), comfort, and ability to reference CT/MRI scans intraoperatively. Read more (The Hindu)
Stryker myMako on Apple Vision Pro Application allowing surgeons to visualize and review detailed 3D surgical plans for Mako robot-assisted joint replacements (pre-surgical planning) using the Apple Vision Pro headset. Read more (Stryker News)
Source Text Ref: Mentioned as a Vision Pro app for pre-surgical planning.
Medivis SurgicalAR on HoloLens 2 FDA-cleared software platform for Microsoft HoloLens 2 that integrates medical imaging (CT, MRI, ultrasound) for pre-operative planning and intraoperative AR guidance during procedures. Uses AI and real-time tracking. Read more (Medivis)
Source Text Ref: Mentioned as specialized software for HoloLens 2 integrating imaging.
SimBioSys TumorSight with Magic Leap Collaboration integrating the TumorSight platform (creates 3D tumor visualizations from standard medical images) with Magic Leap's AR technology for enhanced breast cancer surgery planning. Read more (Business Wire)
Source Text Ref: Mentioned collaboration for breast cancer surgery planning.
Proprio Paradigm Surgical Guidance System AI-powered surgical navigation platform using light field technology and multimodal sensors (cameras) to create real-time 3D visualizations of anatomy and the surgical space without radiation. Focuses initially on spine surgery, providing intraoperative measurements and data capture. Received FDA 510(k) clearance. Read more (Proprio)
ImmersiveTouch Surgical Plan Platform VR platform generating 3D replicas from patient scans (CT/MRI) for surgical planning, team collaboration, and training. Uses headsets like Oculus Rift (Meta Quest) and tools simulating procedures. Used by institutions like Johns Hopkins and University of Chicago. Read more (ImmersiveTouch)
SentiAR Holographic AR Platform AR platform providing surgeons with real-time, hands-free, 3D holographic visualization of patient anatomy (e.g., for cardiac arrhythmias) intraoperatively. Read more (SentiAR)
Stanford Mixed-Reality System for Breast Surgical Planning Developed MR system using Microsoft HoloLens to project 3D holograms from preoperative breast MRI onto the patient, aiming to improve tumor localization and reduce repeat lumpectomies. Pilot study compared MR visualization to standard methods. Read more (Stanford BMR)
Medical Augmented Intelligence (MAI) Body Map AR/VR educational tools providing detailed, anatomically correct 3D human body models for learning and surgical skills practice. AR tools used at Chang Gung Proton Therapy Center for patient positioning. Read more (MAI)
Osso VR's Surgical Training Platform (Osso Health) Offers realistic VR simulations of orthopedic surgeries (e.g., knee/shoulder replacement, carpal tunnel release) on platforms like Meta Quest and Apple Vision Pro (as Osso Health app). Includes assessment and analytics. Proven to improve skill transfer. Read more (Osso VR)
Source Text Ref: VR training for orthopedics on Meta Quest & Apple Vision Pro.
PrecisionOS's VR Training Modules Provides medical-grade VR training simulators for various surgical specialties (orthopedics, general surgery) on platforms like Meta Quest. Focuses on realistic simulation, freedom of movement, and competency-based learning rather than scripted steps. Read more (PrecisionOS)
Source Text Ref: VR training for multiple specialties on Meta Quest.
FundamentalVR's Surgical Training Applications (Fundamental Surgery) Leverages Apple Vision Pro's spatial computing for hands-on, gesture-based training (e.g., ultrasound-guided procedures). Also offers VR platform with haptics on other headsets for various specialties (e.g., ophthalmology via partnership with AAO). Read more (FundamentalVR)
Source Text Ref: Spatial computing training on Vision Pro.
Medicalholodeck on Meta Quest Offers detailed 3D human anatomy models for interactive learning, virtual dissections, rendering DICOM files (CT/MRI) for surgical planning, and collaborative sessions (multiplayer) on Meta Quest headsets. Read more (Medicalholodeck)
Source Text Ref: Anatomy exploration, DICOM rendering, collaboration on Meta Quest.
Immertec on Meta Quest Platform allowing medical professionals to observe live surgical procedures remotely via Meta Quest headsets, interact with the surgeon and other attendees, and view multiple medical feeds. Read more (Meta Store)
Source Text Ref: VR platform for live surgery observation and interaction.
Level Ex Surgical Simulation Games Develops mobile and XR (AR/VR/desktop) medical simulation games for training and education across various specialties, including surgery (e.g., Spine XR game). Read more (Level Ex)
Hospital Clinic Barcelona / Sony VR Collaboration Exploring VR for medical teaching using Sony's NUCLeUS digital OR solution to provide immersive first-hand views of procedures for students without being physically present. Read more (Sony Pro)
